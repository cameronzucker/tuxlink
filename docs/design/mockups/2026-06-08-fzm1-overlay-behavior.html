↑ Actual 1280×800 device frame. Drawer open = radio panel floats over the reader's right ~400px; the message text stays readable on the left. Drawer closed = thin grip strip at the right edge, full-width reader.

1 open detailThe one case overlay can't paint over: the form-viewer webview

When you open a received HTML form (e.g. an ICS-309 you got over the air), Tuxlink renders it in a child Tauri webview pinned over the reading pane — and a child webview always paints above our HTML, so the floating radio panel would be punched through by the form. (That occlusion is exactly why the first attempt chose push.)

The form viewer already tracks a placeholder <div> via a ResizeObserver, so we can make the form yield the drawer's 400px only while the drawer is open — the form shrinks to the left and stays fully visible beside the radio panel; everywhere else it's a true overlay. Alternative: hide the form entirely while the drawer is open. I recommend yield-left (form stays usable for relay). Screen 2 is the bigger question — the left rail.
